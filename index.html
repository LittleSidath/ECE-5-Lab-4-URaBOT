What does PID stand for? What is the purpose of the P, the I, and the D? What steps did you take to choose the best values for your robot? Explain this like you would for someone taking this course next year.

  PID stands for Proportion, Integral, and Derivative. The purpose of the P is to steer harder the farther away a car is from a desired trajectory. The purpose of I is to check whether a car is spending more time on side of the trajectory than the other side. The purpose of D is check how fast we are moving in a perpedicular direction with respect to the desired trajectory. In choosing the values, if the car is overshooting a lot, then the P needs to be lowered and vise versa. If the car is unstable, then the I value needs to be lowered and vise versa. If the car takes a long time for the car to reach the line, then the D value needs to be lowered and vise versa.
